Dear friends and supporters of BEN,

As the year comes to a close, we wanted to take a moment to reflect on all that we have accomplished together this year.

### United Nations SDG Focus

This past year, we have:

- Provided educational resources and support to thousands of students, especially in underserved communities.- Trained and supported teachers and community leaders to effectively teach about blockchain technology inside and outside of the classroom by equipping them with endless educational videos and lesson plans.- Hosted, co-hosted, and spoke at numerous events, conferences, hackathons, and workshops around the world to engage and educate our communities about blockchain.- Partnered with several businesses and organizations that help nonprofits and NGOs flourish through blockchain.
We prioritized four key principles from the [United Nations Sustainable Development Goals](https://www.un.org/sustainabledevelopment/sustainable-development-goals/):

![](https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1024/1*EPhSS4n8bNTvTBhz1W8piA.png)

**SDG 4. Quality Education**

BEN provides courses at beginner, intermediate, and advanced levels for students of any academic background to get up to speed. BEN also has content translated into Spanish, French, Hindi, and more for non-English speaking students from all over the world to learn and take advantage of the opportunities in blockchain.

**SDG 5. Gender Equality**

BEN is especially committed to empowering women to take the lead in this new technological frontier. In 2021, BEN launched a program in Turkey where we rented office space to host a learning center where Turkish students from different universities could come together to learn, discuss, and create innovative projects, all under the leadership of a selected women leader that was fully mentored and supported by BEN.

With the outbreak of the war between Russia and Ukraine, many BEN students were affected in the region. The organization decided to give a job opportunity to one of their female students as the global marketing manager. This opportunity was a key highlight of 2022 for the charity, as the life of this student was dramatically changed; from leaving her home country at war to a new country with all expenses covered, while at the same time being mentored and given a new career opportunity and working 1:1 directly with the BEN team on their global initiatives.

**SDG 8. Decent Work and Economic Growth**

At BEN, management strives to create an environment that provides equal opportunities to all individuals despite their socioeconomic backgrounds. Over the years, the non-profit has provided financial aid, scholarships, flights, Airbnbs, and free conference tickets to hundreds of students from all over the world to attend blockchain conferences and hackathons where they can find new opportunities for jobs or to create their own startups.

**SDG 10. Reduced Inequality**

Starting with just a few college students that shared enthusiasm about this new technology, the network grew over the years to many universities across the nation and worldwide. At BEN, students from any background are welcome to join and represent their community. One of the non-profit’s core principles is to have students from different cultures and walks of life come together to learn, discuss and build wealth for their communities.

From university clubs in the United States to universities across Turkey, Europe, Africa, Latin America, India, and more, BEN is proud to have one if not the most diverse network of students in the world.
